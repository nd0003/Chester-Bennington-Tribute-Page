Chester Bennington

Remembering the talented vocalist

Chester Benningon's Childhood

  • Age 0 - Born on the 20th of March, 1976 in Phoenix, Arizona.
  • Age 7 - Bennington became a victim of sexual abuse from an older male friend. Although his father was a police detective who worked on child sexual abuse cases, Bennington didn't tell anyone what was happening in fear of people thinking he was lying or gay. This abuse continued until he was 13. Many years later, he decided to reveal the identity of the abuser to his father, but did not press charges because he had found out his abuser was also a victim of sexual abuse.
  • Age 11 - Bennington's parents divorced. With the situation he had at home and the abuse, Bennington felt the urge to run away and kill people. Bennington comforted himself by writing songs, poems and drawing pictures. His father gained custody of him after the divorce. Bennington started to abuse alcohol, marijuana, opium, cocaine, meth and LSD due to all the events in his life and being physically bullied in high school. In an interview, Bennington said that he was "knocked around like a rag doll at school, for being skinny and looking different."
  • Age 17 - Bennington decided to move in with his mother. His mother banned him from leaving the house temporarily after finding out about his drug activity. Before pursuing music, Bennington worked at a Burger King.

The Start of Chester Bennington's Musical Career

  • 1993 - Bennington and his friends were part of a band called Sean Dowdell and His Friends. They released a 3 track cassette in the year of 1993.
  • 1993-1998 - Bennington and his friend Dowdell form a new band called "Grey Daze." They released a demo in 1993 and two albums: Wake Me (1994) and No Sun Today (1997). Bennington left Grey Daze in 1998.

The Beginning of Linkin Park

Bennington was frustrated with his career in music and even considered quitting when he was offered an audition with the the members of "Xero" by Jeff Blue, the vice president of artists and repertoire at Zomba Music in Los Angeles. Bennington quit his job at a digital services firm and travelled to California for the audition. Bennington left his birthday party early to record his audition, which won him a position in the band. Bennington was now a member of Xero. Xero made significant progress with Bennington but struggled to find a record deal. After facing multiple rejections, Jeff Blue helped the band sign with Warner. Xero also decided to change their name to "Lincoln Park," but Warner gave them "Linkin Park."
